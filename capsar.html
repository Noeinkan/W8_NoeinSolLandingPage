The Problem

Why BEPs are still broken

Four problems that every BIM manager and information manager recognises.

01

Blank-page paralysis

Every BEP starts from a blank template or a copy of the last one. There’s no structured starting point, so teams spend days just getting the document framework right before writing any actual content.

02

EIRs are dense PDFs

Client requirements are buried in 30–50 page documents with no structure. Extracting what matters — and tracking whether the BEP actually responds to it — is manual and error-prone.

03

No AI for confidential projects

Generic AI tools send your project data to third-party providers. On sensitive infrastructure and defence projects, that’s a non-starter. Teams need AI assistance without the privacy risk.

04

ISO 19650 expertise assumed, not embedded

Templates assume the author already knows the standard inside out. They don’t explain what each section needs, what good looks like, or how to align with the specific EIR. Expertise should be in the tool, not assumed from the user.
