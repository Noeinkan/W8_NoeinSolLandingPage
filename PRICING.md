# Pricing Rationale

Internal record of how the published prices on [services.html](services.html) were set, what they're benchmarked against, and how to evolve them. Not for publication.

Last reviewed: 2026-04-25.

## The strategic context (read this first)

Andrea is moving from a £80k permanent role to private contracting for the first time, with an aim of gradually detaching rather than a hard quit. That single fact reshapes how the published prices should be read:

- **The £80k income is leverage, not a constraint.** It removes runway pressure and means contract #1 should be optimised for *information* — what the market actually pays for this profile — not for income.
- **The site and the near-term goal are two different products.** The site sells boutique consulting (fixed-fee BEPs, retained advisor, scoped engagements) to inbound buyers. That's a 12–18 month brand play. What pays the bills first is **6–12 month contract roles via recruiters** (UK or EU, inside or outside IR35). Recruiters don't read services pages.
- **Published prices are an anchor, not a commitment.** The £650/day on the site is a public signal that we want consistent with the CV submitted to recruiters. The actual quote can be whatever the role's budget allows; the published number's job is to not undersell.

## How each number was set originally

**£650/day** — chosen as a senior-but-accessible day rate. Anchors the fixed fees below.
**£2,500 pre-appointment BEP** ≈ 4 days at £650 (delivered in 5–7 days; tooling absorbs the gap).
**£3,500 post-appointment BEP** ≈ 5.4 days at £650 (extra TIDP/MIDP and execution-plan sections).
**£1,500 EIR advisory** ≈ 2.3 days at £650 (low-friction wedge into BEP work).

The numbers are internally coherent: day rate × estimated effort, with Capsar.io as margin.

## The take-home floor (why £650 isn't arbitrary)

Rough arithmetic for a first-time contractor coming from £80k permanent (≈ £56k take-home after tax/NI):

| Setup | Day rate to match £56k take-home | Day rate for meaningful uplift |
|---|---|---|
| Inside IR35 (umbrella) | ~£500/day | £600–650/day (~£70–80k take-home) |
| Outside IR35 (Ltd Co, optimised) | ~£400/day | £600/day (~£90–100k take-home) |

Below those floors, contracting is a pay cut. £650/day inside IR35 lands at a meaningful uplift; £750+ outside IR35 starts looking like a real income jump. Numbers depend on umbrella fees, pension contributions, expenses — confirm with an accountant before signing anything.

## Where £650 sits in the market band

Day rates Andrea has observed for similar roles: **£450–£950**. Decoded:

| Band | Typical role |
|---|---|
| £450/day | Junior or regional, inside IR35, short-term cover |
| £550–£650 | Solid mid-senior BIM/IM, often inside IR35 |
| £700–£800 | Senior outside IR35, infrastructure / regulated / specialist |
| £850–£950 | Niche specialist via boutique agencies, almost always outside IR35 |

Andrea's profile (HS2, Heathrow, RICS-Certified BIM Professional, ISO 19650 specialism, Autodesk 40 Under 40) genuinely fits **£700–£800 outside IR35**. **But** recruiters benchmark against the *role's budgeted rate*, not the candidate's CV. A role budgeted at £550 inside IR35 doesn't bridge to £900 no matter how strong the profile.

## Strategy for year one

1. **Hold the published £650 as a list rate.** Top of mid-senior, signals seniority, doesn't scare procurement. Match what's on the CV submitted to recruiters.
2. **Get on the recruiter circuit now.** Hays Construction & Property, Randstad C&PE, Morson, Vivid. Available for part-time / 3-days-a-week to enable progressive transition.
3. **Treat first three quote conversations as the real benchmark.** Three rejections at £650 = it's high. Three instant accepts = it's low. Beats every salary guide.
4. **Quote ranges, not points, for boutique enquiries.** "From £650/day" or "£650–£900 depending on scope" leaves room for premium scopes without bespoke quoting.
5. **Treat the site as the long compounding play.** It will not drive deal flow in year one. It exists for credibility when a recruiter or client Googles Andrea mid-quote conversation.

## What to update from real signal

After the first 3–5 recruiter or client conversations, update this doc with:

- Actual rates quoted to / accepted by Andrea (and IR35 status of each role)
- Which channels produced leads (recruiter A vs recruiter B vs inbound vs network)
- Which fixed-fee offers attracted enquiries vs which were ignored
- Any objection patterns ("too high", "not specialist enough", "we use [competitor]")

Revise published prices only after this signal lands. Don't revise from speculation.

## When to revisit (cadence)

- **Quarterly** during year one, to fold in real signal
- **Annually** thereafter, against the next HAUS / ContractorUK rate guide
- **Immediately** if any of: a quote is rejected on price; a quote is accepted in under 24h; a major credential is added (ISO 19650-2 individual certification); Capsar.io ships as a public product (changes the BEP fee model)

## What's deliberately not in the published prices

- **No EUR equivalents on the IT site.** EU contract rates differ structurally; Italy specifically runs €350–€500/day for senior BIM. Worth a separate conversation if EU/IT contract leads materialise.
- **No tier-1-contractor pricing tier.** £2,500–£3,500 BEPs are right for SME tier-2/3. Tier-1 main contractors absorb 2–3× without flinching — add an "enterprise on request" option only if those leads start coming in.
- **No premium senior rate.** The site doesn't yet expose a £900/day specialist tier. Hold until there's at least one signed engagement to anchor the claim.
